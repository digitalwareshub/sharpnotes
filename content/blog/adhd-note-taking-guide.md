## The 4 ADHD Brain Challenges That Destroy Note-Taking

### **Challenge 1: Working Memory Issues**

**Neurotypical brain:** Holds 7¬±2 items in working memory

**ADHD brain:** Holds maybe 3-4 items (on a good day)

**What this means for notes:**

You're in a meeting. Someone says:
> "Email Sarah about the Q4 budget, call Tom about the timeline, update the roadmap doc, and schedule a follow-up for next Tuesday."

**Neurotypical person:** Writes down all 4 things

**ADHD person:** 
- Starts writing "Email Sarah..."
- Forgets what comes next
- "Wait, what was I supposed to email Sarah about?"
- Gives up, writes "follow up with Sarah"
- Forgets the other 3 things entirely

**Result:** Incomplete notes, missed tasks, anxiety

---

### **Challenge 2: Object Permanence**

**The problem:** Out of sight = doesn't exist

You took notes. You saved them. You'll "organize them later."

**Later never comes** because the moment you close that file, it ceases to exist in your mind.

**Neurotypical approach:** "I'll review my notes tonight"

**ADHD reality:** 
- Tonight: "What notes?"
- Next week: "Oh shit, I had notes for that meeting"
- Next month: *discovers notes while searching for something else*

---

### **Challenge 3: Executive Dysfunction**

**The planning/doing gap:**

**What you plan:** 
1. Take notes during meeting
2. Review notes after meeting
3. Extract action items
4. Add to task list
5. Organize by project

**What actually happens:**
1. Take notes during meeting ‚úì
2. ~~Review notes after meeting~~ (forgot)
3. ~~Extract action items~~ (never happened)
4. ~~Add to task list~~ (what task list?)
5. ~~Organize by project~~ (too many steps, brain shut down)

**The gap between "I should do this" and "I am doing this" is HUGE with ADHD.**

---

### **Challenge 4: Hyperfocus ‚Üí Info Overload**

**Two modes:**
1. Can't focus, write nothing
2. Hyperfocus, write EVERYTHING

**Mode 2 is the problem.**

You're hyperfocused in a meeting. You write:
- 2,000 words
- Every tangent
- Every side conversation
- Every idea that popped into your head

**Later:** You have a novel. No idea what's important. Too overwhelming to review.

**Result:** Never look at it again.

## Why Traditional Note Systems Fail for ADHD

### **System: Bullet Journaling**

**The promise:** "Just draw some boxes and use symbols!"

**The ADHD reality:**

**Day 1:** Beautiful setup. Color-coded. Perfectly organized.

**Day 3:** Forgot to do it.

**Day 7:** Feel guilty. Try to catch up. Overwhelmed.

**Day 10:** Abandon completely.

**Why it fails:** 
- Requires daily consistency (ADHD kryptonite)
- If you skip a day, you feel behind forever
- Too many steps (draw boxes, use symbols, transfer tasks)

---

### **System: Notion / Complex Databases**

**The promise:** "One system for everything!"

**The ADHD reality:**

**Week 1:** Spend 10 hours building the perfect system
- Custom databases
- Linked relations
- Beautiful templates

**Week 2:** Overwhelmed by options
- "Which database do I use for this note?"
- "Wait, how do I create a new entry?"
- Analysis paralysis

**Week 3:** Stop using it

**Why it fails:**
- Setup paralysis (perfectionism meets executive dysfunction)
- Too many decisions (which database? what template?)
- Requires remembering your own system

---
